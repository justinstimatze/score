## LINEUP 1 (Sessions 1–25)

**1. Magic, Mayhem and Money – Lessons from The Alchemist**
Francine Boon & Victor van Doorn (Sherlocked) — 4-year, 38-person immersive live game. Pre-sales funding formula; how to keep a large team aligned on a shared picture.

**2. Human Tech Under Martial Law: Lightsticks, Flags, Prepayment & BARAM**
Jooseok Oh (BARAM Experience Co.) — South Korea 2024 martial law night: K-pop lightsticks, meme flags, prepaid coffee as bottom-up "human technologies." Design with what audiences invent for themselves, not just tools you build.

**3. Designed to Deceive – Turning Nature's Deception Into Revenue-driving IP at the International Spy Museum**
Nathan Adkisson (Local Projects) — Camouflage exhibition at DC Spy Museum. Data as creative fuel; broad topic made ownable through immersive design + personal recognition.

**4. The Presence Economy: Designing for a World-First Reality**
James Allsopp (PRELOADED) — Moving from screen-first to world-first design. Wearables, ambient tech, "Intent-Aware Interactions" and "Shared-World Companions." Six affordances of wearable play.

**5. Worlds Not Walls: 7 Principles for Turning Attractions Into Playable Worlds**
Richard Winter (PRELOADED) — PXD framework: "Low Thresholds, Wide Walls, High Ceilings." Shift from showing a story to hosting an ecosystem. Diegetic instruction over tutorials; Eyes-Up Play.

**6. When Retail Becomes Experience**
Rory McCloskey & Gen Cleary (Smartech / The HUM) — TM:RW stores (London, Berlin, Rome, NYC) + The HUM installation. In-store experience as emotional connection before purchase; pathways from store to home.

**7. Designing With Music and Sound: Creating Experiences That Transform**
Gen Cleary (Sound Tech Connect Corp / The HUM) — Music and sound as foundational, not secondary. Body-first approach; dosage checklist to avoid overstimulation; haptics safety playbook.

**8. I Must Be Invisible: An In/Visibility Lab Performance Lecture**
Gabrielle Senza (Studio Lab Eleven) — Performance lecture on invisibility as lived experience. Where visibility feels empowering vs. risky; how being seen/unseen shapes behavior and belonging.

**9. Future Entertainment Destinations**
Val Syganevich (Freckled Sky) — "Experiences are not finished objects, but evolving worlds." Adaptable (story grows), Dynamic (always adding), Interactive (guests become plot), Personalized (every journey unique), Hyperreal (reality and digital fuse).

**10. The Direct Approach: An Olivier Award Winner Bringing a Directorial Lens to Events**
Donnacadh O'Briain — 20 years stage direction applied to corporate events, L&D, LBE, attractions. What a director actually does; transferability of skills outside comfort zones.

**11. Zombies to Zeitgeist: How Immersive Experiences Became Culturally Resonant**
Sarah Morris (Immersive Ideas Ltd) — Immersive went mainstream via zombies because audiences already knew the world's rules. Repeatable cultural design framework; when cultural references strengthen vs. weaken immersion.

**12. This Has Never Been Done Before: Producing the Unknown**
Sherri Nevins (Autodesk) — Immersive producers work without playbooks. Frameworks for first-of-kind decisions; how to tame scope creep; Would You Rather / Hot Ones / "The Producer's Line" formats.

**13. Journeys and Spectacles**
Gerred Blyth (PSB / experience gift consultant) — UX/product direction by day, show designer by night. 6 principles of stage design that apply to apps; 10 principles of product leadership that apply to shows.

**14. If Not an Experience, Then What?**
Scott Schoeneberger (Bluewater) — The Experience Amplifier™: five pillars (attention, emotion, surprise, agency, momentum) to design moments that stick, spread, and spark action.

**15. Unfinished Endings: A Living Archive of Love and Loss**
Brooke Viegut (for.play / Odyssey Works) — Co-created community installation: cardboard fortress, visitor recordings of heartaches, AI-augmented collective witnessing. Low-cost (salvaged materials, phones/iPads); grief in experience design.

**16. Engineering Emotion, Memory, and Meaning**
Alexandra Robinson (CarbonBlack Technology) — Holographic media and dark projection surfaces. Restraint creates memorability; contrast creates clarity; depth drives presence; meaning emerges from absence, not stimulation.

**17. Let's Be Frank: Why the Experience Economy Doesn't Need More Meaningless Innovation**
Tommy Lexen (Frank Kelly Consultancy) — Organizations fail at innovation by performing it. Four Honest Questions Framework; Early Kill Indicators; when a pilot won't scale to a platform.

**18. ROI, ROX & Timing: An Experiential Love Triangle**
Kris Nalla (DreamCrew Entertainment / Luna Luna) — Two real Luna Luna outcomes: a win (5-figure validation before venue/show design) and a loss (high-volume show locked too early). Sequencing audience learning, capital, and commitment determines ROI.

**19. Channeling / Accepting Grief to Make a Fun Experience**
Steve Tiseo (Friendly Vengeance) — Building a fun, educational cancer exhibition two years after losing his mother to cancer. How personal scars fuel creation; using experiences to confront grief.

**20. Crafting Immersive Worlds With Holoperception Dynamics**
Martin Taylor (flux.space) — Immersive experiences operate like dreams, not cinema. Novel framework: dream-logic navigation; Perception Stack (attention → embodiment → spatial orientation → interaction → meaning); metaphor as navigation.

**21. Invitation To Play: How Gaming Experiences Drive Fandom**
Ashley Wagner (Supercell) — Brawl Stars: Welcome To Starr Park. Gaming principles in live: multi-level engagement (casual → engaged → superfan); shared missions; measuring belonging, not bodies through door.

**22. Psychotechnology**
Jasmine Bina (Concept Bureau) — The future is shaped by invisible mental models, meaning systems, and narratives. Three tools: Big Ideas (unconscious values), Market Conditioning (normalizing and scaling values), Units of Culture (outdated frameworks = latent demand).

**23. Designing the Arc of Emotion: Wildly Human Experiences Through Music and Visuals**
George Berlin & Jeena Earthiva — Music as main character. Emotional arc: plant → nurture → grow. Key Change as inner shift. Shadow & Light; game theory of withholding reward.

**24. Lessons Learned From a Mouse (No, Not That One)!**
TJ Schier (SmashSwing) — 18 years at Chuck E. Cheese's, then BigShots Golf → sold. How to avoid the year-2 sales nosedive in competitive socializing venues; pillars for repeat traffic.

**25. Intentional Experiences in the Transformation Economy**
Ramy Elnagar (White Mirror) — Beyond Pine's Experience Economy into Transformation Economy. "Spaces that get closer": designed from nervous system out, ritual and habit formation, identity/meaning. Return on Transformation as metric. Behavioral/physiological outcomes.

## LINEUP 2 (Sessions 26–50)

**26. Give a Man a Record & They Dance for the Day. Give That Man a Synthesizer and They Dance for a Lifetime**
Zenon Olenski (designerzen limited) — Music automation history + AI's future. Co-creation to prevent full automation of humanity; accessible Sympathetic Synthesizer software.

**27. Designing Belonging: How The Long Road Built a Community, Not Just a Festival**
Baylen Leonard (The Long Road Festival) — UK's largest outdoor country music festival (30k capacity), 7 years old. World-building turns passive attendees into active community; intimate spaces (The Front Porch) within large-scale events; bridging culture gaps.

**28. How to Prototype, Playtest, and Prove Million-Dollar Experiences (Before You Build a Nightmare)**
Sandesh Somani (Visceral) — Cardboard prototypes to catch broken mechanics before opening night. Rapid Prototype Framework; Playtest Observation Guide; Stakeholder Proof Toolkit.

**29. Destination as Producer: How DMOs Fund, Scale, and Prove Partner-led Experiences Without Owning Them**
Seth Lieber (Seth Lieber Creative LLC) — Destination-Experience Flywheel (pipeline → readiness → investment → distribution → measurement). DMO as producer, not owner. ROI/ROX dashboard template.

**30. From Closets to Multiverses**
Nic Kessler, Kierna Conner & Sage Starkey (Hawkmoon) — Work on Universal Epic Universe's Monsters Unchained + Dark Universe (THEA winners 2025) and smaller intimate experiences. Intimate → epic; audience at center; feedback loop for communal experiences.

**31. Humans Immerse Humans: Why the Most Critical Layer of Live Events Is Still Left to Chance**
Terence Leclere (MetaforYou) — The human interaction layer determines whether guests immerse or disengage — and is almost always an afterthought. Guests seek permission to immerse; humans grant that permission most effectively. Interpersonal Dynamics Design (IDD) framework; HITs (Human Interactive Touchpoints). Netflix, HBO, Prime, Peacock work.

**32. Spatial Storytelling: Designing Narrative-Driven Exhibitions**
Laura Micalizzi — Framework: abstract concept → coherent exhibition narrative through space, sequence, material. Italian-Japanese cultural platform (Tenoha) case studies; cross-cultural design strategies.

**33. The Great Unplugging: Why Digital Backlash is an Opportunity for Experiences**
Dean Rodgers (Secret Cinema) — Peak social media was 2022; we're on the downslide. AI slop pushes commercial strategy to live. Boom incoming for IRL experience — but only if ready. How to reach audiences without social media.

**34. Build a World: Designing for Future Technicians**
Theresa Macaulay (Science Museum Group) — Workshop: creating immersive worlds-of-work for 11-16 year olds. Audience insight → playful digital environments; live human interaction in digital exhibitions.

**35. One-Night-Stand Events vs. Lifelong Regulars: Which One Is Your Brand Really Hosting?**
Kyle Kocinski (Less Boring) — "If your event vanished next year, who would fight to bring it back?" Diagnostic scorecard; budget grid (cut spectacle, reinvest in repeat behavior); 30-day post-event follow-up flow.

**36. Experience as Gift, Experience as Portal: Designing for a Lover (1-to-1)**
Amy Segreti (Our Circuitries) — Bespoke 1-to-1 sessions on designing intimate/erotic/relational experiences. Communication, sexuality, kink, any realm of intimacy. Practical design for couplehood.

**37. RAGEher: A Space for Women and Femmes to Feel It All**
Kristina McMenamin (askHUH? experiences) — Movement and embodiment experience: talk it out, punch it out, primal scream, DANCE. Anger as fuel. Tools for moving stuck feelings through body.

**38. The Problem Isn't the Tech. It's How We Design Experience Around It**
Lauren Dyer (Brave Duck And Robots) — Technology has optimized experiences but not deepened them. Experience Stack Framework (Intent → Emotion → Behaviour → Touchpoints → Technology). Invisible Tech Decision Filter; Sensory Priority Template; technology as choreographer of feeling, not hero.

**39. Elvis Evolution: Lessons From a Data-led Redesign**
Joe Timson (Cavea) — Elvis Evolution launched to polarized reactions; Layered Reality redesigned using second-by-second emotion analytics. Emotion analytics as diagnostic tool; IP risk in immersive formats; fandom management.

**40. Journey Into the Unknown: When There is No Map**
Catherine Turp (Moment Factory) — Creative leaders asked to make work that doesn't exist yet while stakeholders demand certainty. "Compass of Amazing" to hold direction without defaulting to complacency or over-process. Collective creative instinct as a shared tool.

**41. Know What You're In: Designing Immersive Experiences from a Central Preoccupation**
Hannah Price (Buried Giants) — One clear idea (the central preoccupation) shapes every decision: agency, tone, technology, format, budget, narrative. The Gunpowder Plot case study. Technology decisions start from story needs, not tools. Top-down consistency framework.

**42. High Tech, Low Tech, No Tech: Patterns Observed Across 800+ Immersive Works**
Katrina Lat (No Proscenium) — 800+ immersive works across 3 continents. AURA Framework: Agency, Universe, Resonance, Artistry. Tech alone doesn't determine quality; recurring strengths and common pitfalls across formats.

**43. Experiences That Matter: Building Brand Impact Through Collaboration and Insight**
Balpreet Mangat (TRO) — TRO + BMW 20-year relationship. Retention is 5x more cost-efficient than acquisition; deep trusted partnerships as superpower for growth. [Also appears as #93]

**44. Vita Nova: A Live/Metaverse Disability Musical That Got 7-figure Funding**
Christopher Morrison (Reality+) — Disability justice as creative driver; crip-time methodology; off-the-shelf tech to bring live events to people who can't attend. Doris Duke Foundation project; 7-figure funding through long-term relationship building.

**45. The Experience Economy of Sales: Four Pillars for Selling Your Service**
Andy Ayim (Ayim Limited) — Service-based sales for consultants/freelancers/agencies: content attraction, network activation, curated gatherings, thoughtful outreach. No ad spend.

**46. Scaling the Wild: How Port Lympne Expanded With Nighttime Storytelling**
Rob Paul (LCI Productions) — Safari park's first seasonal light trail (2025). Animal welfare constraints; natural terrain; new guest behaviors; conservation ethos at scale without overextending resources.

**47. The Gift of Experiences: Designing Real Value for Audiences**
Jasmin Jodry (MOTO) — "I design immersive experiences as gifts." Shift from spectacle to catalyst creates joy, connection, belonging. Social remedy for anxiety/loneliness era; designing for outcomes, not spectacle; emotional resonance as ROI.

**48. Using AI to Clarify Experience Design**
Brett Bagenstose (NeoPangea) — AI as design assistant, NOT shortcut or replacement. When and why AI is used during concept development and alignment — and when deliberately not used. Failed experiments included. Framework for purposeful AI use preserving authorship.

**49. Cultural Context as a Narrative Map**
Amr Ellaithy (TCS) — Local culture is not decorative — it's primary structure. Cultural Context Canvas; Narrative Mapping Method; Context-to-Design Checklist; Cultural Misalignment Red Flags.

**50. Music Makes the People**
Stefan Weil (Atelier Markgraph) — Music beyond entertainment: trigger, stimulant, balance, healer, connector, change agent.

## LINEUP 3 (Sessions 51–75)

**55. Designing for Reality: Using Digital Twins to De-risk Live Experiences**
Michael Libby (Worldbuildr) — Digital twins as decision engines, not demos. Live demo built in real time with audience input. Pre-build simulation workflow; stakeholder-friendly tech ROI scorecard.

**56. How to Pitch (Almost) Anything: Craft Yours in 60 Minutes**

**57. Tug of War: The Dance Between Creative and Finance**
David Shulman (Brilliantwork LLC) — Panel: how creatives, investors, and finance leaders approach projects differently. Creative compromise; real perspective from all three sides.

**58. Experience Design for a Better World**
Abraham Burickson (Odyssey Works) — Experience designers uniquely suited to create social impact. Landfall Labs researchers on Environments, Interactions, and Narratives as tools for conditioning what's possible. "Design for change in three areas of Experience Culture."

**59. You're Cordially Invited… NPS is Ready to Retire!**
Romy Alexandra, Maria Niederwieser & Craig Tindall — NPS flattens meaningful experiences to a single number, misguides budgets. Retirement party format. New framework: capture true impact, not just likelihood to recommend.

**60. What Experience Designers Can Learn From Art & Technology**
Marie du Chastel (ikii) — 15 years curating interactive/immersive art. Most impactful experiences come from art + tech worlds, not traditional experience design. Multisensory principles (sound, space, body, interaction, XR) from artistic practice.

**61. Delivering Big Impact Through Smart Design**
Rob Paul (LCI Productions) — Windsor Great Park Illuminated 2025: 250,000 visitors. Value engineering without compromising vision; stakeholder alignment on scale vs. perceived value; audience behavior and dwell time data informing design.

**62. F Transformation; Design for Emergence (Yours and Theirs)**
Kristina McMenamin (askHUH? experiences) — Transformation is not a destination, it's a lifelong practice of emergence. Evolating framework (Dr. Judith Wright). Creating "transformative experiences" without transforming yourself is hypocrisy. 5 micro design interventions for emergence.

**63. Robot Dreams: A Deep Dive Into the Past, Present, and Future of Robotics in Entertainment**
Brian Paiva (Octopus Design Studio) interviewing andyRobot — Lifetime of robotics in entertainment: Drake, Lady Gaga, Bon Jovi, NASA, MTV, Apple. Modern industrial robot capabilities; AI integration; software for non-programmers.

**64. Beyond the Individual Experience: Mapping Experience Ecosystems**
Ilze Mertena (Manchester Metropolitan University) — High streets and cultural districts: micro businesses good at meaningful experiences but struggle to translate to growth. Network analysis for ecosystem mapping; gaps and collaboration opportunities.

**65. An Unfiltered Showdown Between a CCO and Her AI Co-worker**
Anam Ahmad (The Hanging House) — Live conversation between CCO and her AI employee "FIRE" of 2 years. AI returns work "technically correct but spiritually dead." CCO brings instinct/emotion; AI brings logic/speed/alarming lack of nuance. The real tension inside modern creative teams.

**66. Immersive Influence: What if Places of Escape Could Help Rebuild the World We're Escaping From?**
Richard Maddock (Kay Elliott) — White paper launch: experiences as behavioural infrastructure. 3R Framework: **Revelation → Reinforcement → Replication**. Experiences influence norms and behaviors in everyday life; downstream effects rival operational impact. Systems thinking + behavioral science.

**67. Reimagining Live Experiences in the Avatar Era**
Naf A (Leeds-Beckett University) — IRL avatar experiences (masked bands, tribute shows, lifelike avatars) as cultural testbeds for new presence. "Politics of likeness" — IP ownership, licensing, ethical risks. Future: avatars, AI performers, non-human elements holding audience trust.

**68. Designing Mission Days: A Creative Playbook for Global Internal Events**
Claudia Stephenson (INVNT) — Wise's Mission Days: 6,500+ employees, rapidly multiplying locations. Repeatable creative framework, global content spine + local layers, embedding values into staging without just slides. [Also appears as #75]

**69. Unbuilt: What We Can Learn From Our Most Precious Projects**
Geoff Thatcher (Creative Principals) — Dead projects: Disney's Mineral King ski resort, Turner Entertainment Experience (killed by AOL/Time-Warner merger). Workshop format: share dead projects → vote on one → redesign it for 21st century live.

**70. In the Absence of Narrative**
Klaus Sommer Paulsen (AdventureLAB) — What happens when personal/community narratives are erased or inverted? Story experience designer as healer. Case studies in healthcare, community, urban design. How to design a way back to narrative agency.

**71. Big IPs, Small Spaces**
Natalia Bakhlina (Leisure Development Partners) — Can small experiences attract global IP giants? Does it work financially? Business case comparison: big IP vs. regular IP vs. no IP. No one-size-fits-all answer.

**72. Social Has Bibles. Experiences Have… Briefs?**
Riaz Farooq (Pixel Artworks) — Brands polish social guidelines and brand books but have vague "make it amazing" briefs for experiences. Solution: immersive experience brand guidelines defining emotional intent, story DNA, participation expectations, friction tolerances.

**73. Architecting Never-Ending Experiences**
Eliran Farhi (Neoja) — Live AI brainstorming: several human brains + AI in 30 minutes. Hive-Mind Canvas; Evolution Framework (mutate, select, adapt). AI-powered group creativity pitch competition.

**74. The Awkward Antidote: Cultivating Connection from the Kick-off**
Romy Alexandra & Tom Goulooze — Expert facilitators: "connection before content." Day 1 of conference: live meta-cognitive experience on building belonging. No awkward networking, no cringy icebreakers. "Connection before content" made IRL.

**75. Mission Days: Turning 6,500 Employees into a Global Community of "Wisers"**
Claudia Stephenson (INVNT) — Wise's São Paulo Mission Day case study. 5 core approaches for continuous employee engagement in high-growth organizations; active members/advocates vs. passive audience; balancing global consistency with local expression. [See also #68]

Amy Segreti (Our Circuitries) — BRIDGE practice: Biopsychosocial, Responsive, Invitational, Devotional, Generous & Emergent. Designing for nervous system + relational field + emergent moment. Scale without losing intimacy. Responsiveness vs. control.

**83. Making Meaning, Connection, and Transformation: Tools, Reflections, and Explorations**

Abigail Taylor-Sansom (SCAD) — Story redefined as a collection of rules, not linear plot + characters. Works for linear, branching, and open-world equally. Designed specifically for immersive storytelling; not repurposed from film/TV tools. The first narrative tool developing stories as rule sets.

**85. The Long Arc of Immersive Storytelling: Designing Narrative Continuity Across Every Touchpoint**

**86. From Experience to Impact: The GenXP Framework**

**87. The Convergence of Immersive Digital Experiences and Retail Media Networks**
Brad Koerner (Koerner Design) — Immersive environments + retail media networks converging: physical spaces as responsive, data-informed experiences. Architectural surfaces as programmable, personalized, measurable. Beyond "wow" toward engagement and provable impact.

**88. What's the Point of Business Events Anymore?**
Benjamin Hamer (ThinkerTank) — ThinkerTank × Saxton "Future of Business Events" research. People busy, budgets tight: is this worth my time? What creates moments that matter; what's no longer working; designing better event value.

**89. Puppetry as a Gateway to Participation in Immersive Experiences**
Matthew Purdon (Woander) — Puppets from The Wylding Woods. Not puppetry technique — how puppets communicate permission, safety, and curiosity across mixed-age audiences. Felt understanding of invitation vs. performance; tools for lowering audience anxiety.

**90. One To Many: Self-producers Roundtable**
Barbara Ann Michaels (Jester Of The Peace / HumorVille) — Running your own experience as a soloist. How do self-producers team, stabilize, energize, and grow? Community for solo operators.

**91. The Freedom Tower: Transforming Miami's Oldest Skyscraper**

**92. The Product Marketing Mindset: Optimizing Event Impact**

**93. Experiences That Matter: Building Brand Impact** [duplicate of #43]
Balpreet Mangat (TRO / BMW)

**95. Art as a Catalyst for Change**

**96. Why Narrative Drives Immersion, and Should**
